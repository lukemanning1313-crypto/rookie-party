Vet's Playbook
Door Tax first. Pick from The Menu in any order. Send-Off when it's time to go.

Door Tax

Every rookie, no exceptions
Rookie walks up to the door. Handed a beer. Shotguns it on the doorstep before he's allowed inside. Last rookie to show up shotguns two.

The Menu

Vets pick what's next — any order, as many as you want
The Mo-Bettah's Run
Each team sends one rookie to Mo-Bettah's to grab a side of sauce (vet's choice). Clock starts when they leave — send them at separate times so neither knows the other's pace. Slower rookie's team gets a punishment (vets decide).
The Coliseum
Everyone to the balcony. One rookie per team goes to the parking lot. 10 spins on the bat, vet pitches from 15 feet.
Pitch 1: Softball
Pitch 2: Tennis ball
Pitch 3: Ping pong ball
Losing team's rookie chugs in the parking lot while the balcony decides his punishment
Wheelbarrow Send
Two rookies per team pair up. Both chug a beer first, then wheelbarrow race across the parking lot. Losing pair chugs again. Switch positions and run it back.
Blindfold Races
Two rookies blindfolded at one end of the parking lot. First to touch the far wall wins (~200 ft). Vets on the balcony shout directions — or send them the wrong way. Loser chugs.
Water Balloon Drop
Rookie stands in the parking lot. Vets line up on the balcony with water balloons. Rookie has to walk from one end to the other — no running, no flinching. Runs = start over + chug.
The Supply Run
Each team sends a rookie to bring back a cup of flour. The gamble — knock on a neighbor's door and hope someone's home, or sprint to the gas station and guarantee it. Wrong call costs time. Losing team chugs.
The Gauntlet Walk
Vets line the hallway. One rookie at a time walks through. Each vet gives a task as you pass — chug, pushup, answer a question, whatever they feel like. Stop moving = back to the start.
Captain's Pick
Each captain points at one rookie from the other team. 1v1 chug race. Loser's entire team does 15 burpees while the winners watch.
Flip Cup
Team vs team. Best of 3. Normal, then non-dominant hand, then blindfolded. Losing team: penalty shot + 20 pushups each.
Drink Roulette
Line up identical cups — most are beer, a few are loaded with whatever vets find in the fridge. Hot sauce, pickle juice, condiments. Every rookie picks a cup and sends it. No smelling, no switching.
Hot Seat
One rookie at a time. Vets rapid-fire whatever they want — personal questions, team trivia, "roast yourself," rank your teammates. Wrong answer or hesitation = drink. Vets vote on who had the weakest seat — that rookie gets a punishment.

Punishments

Stack these onto any activity
Cold Call
Rookie opens his own contacts. Vets scroll and pick a name. He calls them on speaker, stays on for 60 seconds, and explains what's happening. Can't hang up first.
The Confession
Rookie unlocks his phone and hands it to a vet. Vet picks a conversation and reads the last 10 messages out loud to the room. No context, no explanation.
The Mixer
Vets make a drink using whatever's in the fridge. Rookie chugs the whole thing. No questions about what's in it.
Social Media Takeover
Rookie hands over his Instagram. A vet posts something to his story. Rookie doesn't get to see it until after the party.
The Billboard
Rookie goes to the balcony and yells whatever the vets tell him to. Full volume. Whole parking lot hears it.

The Send-Off

When it's time to go
The Reckoning
Vets announce the losing team from initiation. Losing team lines up — each rookie gets one final punishment from the list above (vets choose). Winning team watches from the balcony.
Balcony Send-Off
Everyone on the balcony. Group shotgun. Group photo. Captains have 30 seconds to say something. Then march to the bus — 2:00 sharp. Not on the bus, you're walking.
